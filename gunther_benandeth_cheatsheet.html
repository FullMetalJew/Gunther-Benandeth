Gunther Benandeth
Human · Fighter 3 · Blademaster Specialist · Bounty Hunter
Blademaster Adaptive Fighting Hit-and-Run
How to play Gunther — the simple version

Gunther is a hit-and-run melee striker. He gets in, does damage, and gets out before anyone can hit him back. He is not a tank — he survives by never being where the enemy expects. Between his Hidden Blade, Adaptive Fighting tricks, and Cunning Strategist's free Disengage, he can strike and reposition every single turn without provoking a single Opportunity Attack.

Every turn revolves around one question: can I strike and escape safely? The answer is almost always yes. His Action Surge (2/SR) is his biggest burst — save it for priority targets or desperate moments. Parry is his insurance policy — hold it for a hit that would drop him. His Adaptive Fighting charges (3/SR) are easy to forget but powerful: Draw extends his crit range, Stow gives a free Disengage, and Change Up swaps fighting styles on the fly.

The Electrobaton is a situational weapon — its Shocking 13 property forces a DEX save or the target takes extra lightning damage and is Shocked (disadvantage on attacks) until end of their next turn. Swap to it when you want to debuff a dangerous attacker before they swing.

// Combat Protocol
Move in close. Attack with Hidden Blade (+5). Bonus action: Cunning Disengage — move out without triggering Opportunity Attacks. Repeat. Use Electrobaton instead when you want to Shock a dangerous enemy before they attack. Use Draw (Adaptive) to extend crits to 19–20 before a big swing. Use Action Surge to double damage on a priority target. Use Parry (reaction) when a melee hit would drop you. Short rests reset almost everything.

Key Class Features
Bonus Action Cunning Strategist Every Turn
Chosen options: Disengage · Throw Grenade
Disengage — move away after striking without triggering Opportunity Attacks. This is Gunther's default every single turn — strike, disengage, reposition.

Throw Grenade — lob a grenade or mine without using your Action. Lets you get a full attack AND area damage on the same turn.
Default every turn: Attack → Cunning Disengage → move out safely. Swap to Grenade when there's a cluster of enemies to punish.
Special Action Surge 2 / Short Rest
Take one additional full Action this turn
Gunther's biggest single-turn burst. Take a full second Action — attack again, dash, or anything else.
Save for: finishing a priority target, when a target turns its back, or a moment that decides the fight. Don't waste it on a healthy enemy early in a fight.
Reaction Parry 2 / Short Rest · Combat Superiority
Triggered when a melee attack hits you — expend a superiority die to reduce damage
When a melee hit connects, spend a superiority die and subtract the result from the damage taken. This can be the difference between staying up and going unconscious.
Use when a hit would drop you below 8 HP or knock you unconscious. Don't burn it on small hits — hold it for the one that matters.
Weapon Property Electrobaton — Shocking 13 Situational
Triggered on a hit — target makes DEX save DC 13
On a failed save: target takes +1d4 lightning damage and is Shocked until end of their next turn — giving them disadvantage on all attack rolls. The Electrobaton hits slightly less hard than the Hidden Blade normally, but the Shocked condition is a meaningful debuff against high-threat enemies.

Note: Finesse means you can use STR or DEX — stick with STR (+3) since it's higher than DEX (+2).
Swap to Electrobaton when facing a powerful melee attacker — Shocking them before their turn gives your whole party a safer round. Don't use it as your default; the Hidden Blade does more reliable damage.
Object Interaction Adaptive Fighting 3 Charges / Short Rest
Three separate uses — expend 1 charge each
↑ Draw — Drawing a weapon extends your crit range to 19–20 until your next turn starts. Best used before a high-stakes attack.

↓ Stow — Stowing a weapon gives you a free Disengage — no action, no bonus action spent. Frees your bonus action for Second Wind or Throw Grenade on the same turn.

⟳ Change Up — Swap your active Fighting Style mid-combat. Use if the situation shifts from melee to ranged.
Draw before attacking for extended crit range. Stow → move out free → bonus action still available for anything else.
Bonus Action Second Wind 1 / Short Rest
Heal 1d10+3 HP
Free healing that only costs a bonus action — meaning you skip Cunning Disengage that turn. Still worth it when you're getting hit hard. Resets on every short rest.
Use when below ~12 HP. Don't hold it — it comes back on a short rest. If you need it, use it.
Reaction Defiant 1 / Short Rest · Human
When you fail a saving throw — reroll and take the higher result
No action cost — triggered automatically on a failed save. Can turn a save that would disable you for a turn into a passing result.
Best on: CON saves, STR saves vs grapple or shove, or any save that would stun, paralyze, or knock you prone.
